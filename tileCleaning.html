To effectively clean grout without damaging it, steer clear of harsh chemicals like bleach that can degrade sealants. The safest and most efficient method is to use a natural grout cleaner.

Here's a simple recipe we recommend:

● Mix 1/2 cup of baking soda, 1/4 cup of hydrogen peroxide, and 1 teaspoon of dish soap.
● Combine these ingredients well.
● Apply the mixture to the grout and let it sit for 10 minutes.
● Afterward, give it a good scrub and rinse it off.

This gentle yet effective approach will help keep your grout clean and preserved.

The need for professional tile and grout cleaning varies, much like with carpets. Several factors play a role in determining how often this service is needed:

Foot Traffic: The amount of daily foot traffic can significantly impact how quickly your tiles and grout become dirty.
Presence of Children and Pets: Homes with children and pets may require more frequent cleanings due to additional spills and debris.
Regular Maintenance: How often and how well you maintain your tile floors also affects the frequency of professional cleanings.

Considering these factors, a general guideline is to have your tile and grout professionally cleaned once a year. However, in high-traffic areas or homes with pets and children, you might consider more frequent cleanings for optimal maintenance and hygiene.
