OUR UPHOLSTERY CLEANING PROCESS

Step 1: Inspection -

Our upholstery cleaning process starts with a thorough examination by our technicians. They assess the fabric type, color, and age of the furniture to determine the most effective approach.

Step 2: Pre-Spot -

Then, they will spot treat any areas on your couches, chairs, mattresses or other upholstered items that look like they need extra attention.

Step 3: Pre-Spray -

We use a special enzyme spray to break down dirt in high-traffic areas and common spots, ensuring a more thorough cleaning.

Step 4: Deep Cleaning -

After spot treatment, the deep cleaning phase begins. Our upholstery cleaning machine injects hot water and our cleaning solution into the furniture to release dirt deep within the upholstery. Subsequently, it extracts the water to remove the dirt.

Step 5: Post Cleaning Inspection -

In the final step, we conduct an inspection of your upholstery with you to ensure your satisfaction with the cleaning results. Your upholstery will be dry within a few hours.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Whenever possible, we prefer using our powerful hot water extraction cleaning process because it offers the most thorough cleaning. However, certain fabrics may demand a more delicate cleaning approach. In such cases, we will assist you in making a decision that best aligns with your needs and the requirements of your furniture.

Typically, each seating position requires around 10-20 minutes. However, the duration may vary based on the level of fabric soiling and the need for spot removal treatments

The choice of cleaning method for your upholstery depends on the specific fabric. Drying time is influenced by both the cleaning method and the optional application of a protector, with typical dry times ranging from 2-6 hours.

Cleaning upholstery can differ significantly depending on the fabric. Before cleaning, make sure to check the manufacturer's cleaning instructions. Typically, the tag on upholstered furniture will have one of four different letters

• W-You can clean this material using water.
• S-No water. You'll need to purchase a special solvent-based cleaner.
• WS-You can use water or a solvent-based cleaner on this sofa.
• X-Vacuum only.

This information will guide you in selecting the appropriate cleaning method for your upholstery.
