TILE & GROUT CLEANING

Tocleanyourtileandgrout, we specifically scrub each grout line and tile with cleaners designed for these surfaces. We utilize high pressure, connected to a truck-mounted unit, to effectively remove dirt and grime embedded in your grout lines.

?After applying a sealant, it's recommended to use a neutral cleaner on your tile and grout. This helps maintain the sealant's effectiveness. Avoiding bleach and other harsh chemical cleaners is also advised, as they can degrade the sealant

Sealing grout lines is important because, like carpet, grout is porous and absorbs spills and soil. A sealant forms a protective barrier, allowing more time for cleanup and preventing spills from soaking into the grout. The type of sealant you choose will affect its stain resistance

Weadvise waiting 30 minutes before walking on the floor after sealant application, to let it dry. The sealant takes about 24 hours to cure completely, so it's best to avoid any liquid contact during this period

The frequency of cleaning your tile and grout depends on factors such as foot traffic, the presence of children and pets, and your usual floor maintenance routine

Over time, dirt accumulates on tile and grout, leading to a dull appearance and discoloration. Professional cleaning is the most effective way to remove this deeply embedded dirt.

HARDWOOD REFINISHING

Screen and reboots are utilized to rejuvenate hardwood floors by eliminating light scratches and blemishes. We gently abrade the floor using a buffer, which prepares the surface for a fresh coat of polyurethane. This not only restores the shine to dull floors but also provides additional protection against scratches

Screen and reboots offer significant advantages, including prolonging the use of hardwood floors without the need for complete sanding and refinishing. This not only saves money but also preserves the wood, as each refinishing process removes a layer of wood. This technique allows for changes in the floor's sheen, from glossy to matte, for example, revitalizing the appearance of the floor. It's a one-day process, far less costly and disruptive than a full sanding and refinishing

The screen and recoat process only targets the top finish layer, meaning it won't fix deep gouges or change the wood's color. For floors requiring extensive repair or a color change, sanding and finishing are the most effective methods. Note that this process is not suitable for floors finished with hard wax oil, only working on polyurethane-finished f loors

Previously, prefinished hardwood floors couldn't undergo screening and recoating. However, recent technological advancements now allow for the screening and recoating of these types of floors

For maintaining the finish and appearance of polyurethane-finished hardwood floors, we recommend a screen and recoat every 3 to 5 years. This frequency helps in preserving the floors' look without allowing excessive wear.
